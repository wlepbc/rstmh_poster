Supporting information for the heftily-titled poster:

What does the incidence of leprosy diagnosis tell us about underlying incidence and progress towards elimination? Novel methodologies for estimating incidence accounting for the long incubation period.

This poster was presented at the RSTMH biennial meeting in September 2016.

Information about the underlying numbers of undiagnosed sub-clinical and clinical cases and the forecasting of future cases is very important to the management of leprosy control programmes.

We use a Bayesian back-calculation model to fit to the annually reported numbers of diagnoses. Combining the observed numbers of cases with external knowledge about the time periods from:

  • infection to clinical symptom onset, and
  • onset to diagnosis

allows inferences to be made about unobservable sub-clinical and undiagnosed clinical cases.
